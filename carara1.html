Porsche 911 (German: Neunelfer) is the common name of a family of sports cars and GT cars produced by the German Porsche AG company from 1965 to the present. The generations of the family are usually divided into the Porsche 911 Classic, produced in 1964-1989, and the new generation cars, which retained only the general layout and external style with the Classic family. The same body types are common to all cars: two-door, two-seat (2+2) coupe for the main model and convertible, targa, which have been integral in the Porsche model line from 1967 to the present (2024). Starting from the second generation, each generation of cars is produced with its own three-digit index, which from the fourth generation can be considered as an independent car model.
