Porsche Macan is a compact crossover of the German automobile company Porsche, created on the basis of the Audi Q5, was presented on November 20, 2013 at the Los Angeles Auto Show. Initially, the car was supposed to be called the Porsche Cajun (the word "Cajun" was conceived as an acronym for the words "Cayenne Junior" — "junior Cayenne"). However, in February 2012, it was changed to Porsche Macan: the company decided to use the Indonesian word "machan", meaning "tiger". At the same time, its original Indonesian pronunciation was distorted: the letter "c", which sounds in Indonesian as "h — /tʃ/", was voiced in the name of the model in a way more typical for most European languages — as "k — /k/". The official presentation of the Porsche Macan took place at the Los Angeles Auto Show on November 20.
